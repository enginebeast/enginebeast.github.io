Definition of electric potential energy

We know that relationship between potential energy and work can be indicated by following equation. $$ \begin{flalign*} \Delta U = U_f - U_i = -W && \end{flalign*} $$ When two particle which far away from infinite distance close into each other, /(U_i/) become zero and we can define the electric potential energy as follows. $$ \begin{flalign*} U_f = U = -W && \end{flalign*} $$ Of course, infinite distance is not possible in the real world. But, the concept is used to suppose the situation that interation of two particles go to existance from not-existance. It's same as we often to suppose the situation that friction does not exist.

Definition

fold/unfold $$ \begin{flalign*} V = U/q && \end{flalign*} $$ Where \(q_{enc}\) is net charge, and \(\epsilon_0\) is permittivity of free space.

If \(q_{enc}\) is positive, a net flux heads to the outside. And if \(q_{enc}\) is negative, a net flux heads to the inside.
