Definition of electric field

fold/unfold $$ \begin{flalign*} \vec{E} = \frac{\vec{F}}{q_0} && \end{flalign*} $$ Where, \(q_0\) is test charge which become the standard of electric fields.

By the Coulomb's law, we can get the following equation. $$ \begin{flalign*} \vec{E} = \frac{1}{4\pi\epsilon_0}\frac{qq_0}{r^2}\hat{r}\frac{1}{q_0} = \frac{1}{4\pi\epsilon_0}\frac{q}{r^2}\hat{r} && \end{flalign*} $$

The electric field due to a point charge

$$ \begin{flalign*} \vec{E} = \sum^n_{i=1}\vec{E_i} && \end{flalign*} $$

The electric field due to an electric dipole

fold/unfold An electric dipole is a system consisting of two equal and opposite charges, q and -q, separated by certain distance. The equation for electric dipole moment is as follows. $$ \begin{flalign*} \vec{E} = \frac{1}{2\pi\epsilon_0}\frac{qd}{z^3} && \end{flalign*} $$ Where d is a distance between two charge, and z is a distance between center of two charge and a test charge.
